VMware Cloud

Introduction

Overview

This document covers the terminology related to VMware Cloud as well as the prerequisites and tasks required to activate services.

The best strategy for new customers is to browse the Project Plans and become familiar with the absolute minimal requirements to complete the on-boarding process. The supplemental information in this guide will provide context to the tasks listed within the project plans.

Terminology

Fund Owner

A Fund Owner is the individual who is responsible for providing funding for VMware Cloud services. They will be responsible for activating services.

VMware Cloud Organization

The VMware Cloud Organization (Org) may be thought of as a top-level construct which owns 1 or more cloud services. There will be 1 or more users associated with an Org, the first of which being the Fund Owner who activated the Org.

VMware Cloud Users

Users within VMware Cloud may be associated with 1 or more Orgs and will have 1 of 2 roles within an Org:

  • Org Member - Has the ability to manage cloud services to which they have been granted access. If required, Org Members may have their access limited to the following permissions:
    • Billing Read-Only - Restricted to viewing billing information for the Org.
    • Support User - Restricted to managing support issues for the Org.
  • Org Owner - Has the additional ability to manage users within the org.

The Fund Owner will be given the role of Org Owner within the newly created Org. Typically, the first task of the Fund Owner will be to add additional users to the Org. This may be performed from the Identity & Access Management section of the VMC Console. See the related HowTo for specific instructions.

Service Roles

Individual cloud services will typically come with their own sets of roles. These roles are managed under the Identity & Access Management section of the VMC Console and will be configured on a per-user basis. See the related HowTo for specific instructions.

Prerequisites

My VMware Account

VMware Cloud Organizations will have a primary contact, known as the Fund Owner, who is the individual providing funding for VMware Cloud services. Not only must the Fund Owner have an active account on My VMware, but all required fields of the account profile must be populated. Especially important is the email address since it will be used for service activation.

If the account profile of the Fund Owner is not completely populated as required, then activation cannot be completed.

Funding Through My VMware

If services will be funded through a My VMware account then these funds must be in place prior to activation. Only accounts which have been fully funded and associated with the Fund Owner will be able to activate the service and deploy an SDDC. Billing information is available from the My VMware account of the Fund Owner.

Some customers may choose to utilize Subscription Purchase Program (SPP) credits for VMware Cloud. These are considered to be a valid form of funding.

Credit Card Payments

Credit cards are a valid form of payment for VMware Cloud services. If paying by credit card then you will be prompted for payment as part of the activation process.

Support Model

Support in VMware Cloud is broken up into two functions:

  1. Front-end support
  2. Back-end support

Front-end support is delivered in the form of the Cloud Services Engineering team and is directly available via live-chat or by opening a support issue from within the cloud console. For critical support issues, live-chat is the fastest means of accessing the font-end support teams.

Back-end support is delivered in the form of one or more VMware Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) teams. The SRE teams are responsible for managing and maintaining all cloud services, and perform monitoring of all critical infrastructure under their control. The SRE teams are also responsible for performing updates and maintenance to certain components of the various cloud services.

Since VMware Cloud is an as-a-service offering, the support model is designed to be 24x7x365. However, the target response times depend on the severity of the issue. Details on cloud service support policies are available at the following URL:

https://www.vmware.com/support/policies/saas-support.html
